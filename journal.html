Journal

Fall 2025

My first semester in college! This semester came with a lot of responsibility that I didn't quite know how to manage at first. Finding a routine, and learning not to say yes to everything, was something I had to figure out the hard way.

I also struggled a lot with comparison this semester and realized I had a pretty unhealthy relationship with what "success" actually meant. Starting as early as elementary school, so many things were framed as competitions with clear winners: science fair, Battle of the Books, and so on. You learned to measure how well you did by how much you outperformed someone else.

Although I'm still a competitive person (as you can definitely tell from my CliftonStrengths), I started to redefine success for myself. Instead of measuring it against the skill sets of the people around me, I began valuing it based on how much I personally improved. There will always be someone who forms connections more easily than me, someone smarter, someone more fit, someone with better emotional poise, etc. That realization led to a motto that really stuck with me this semester: "Own lane, own race, own pace."

I'm on my own journey, and everyone else is on theirs. My success shouldn't be defined by what they're doing because ultimately, that doesn't determine my future.

Unrelated to all of that, I would highly recommend that every college freshman take a lighter course load their first semester! Spend time exploring campus and getting involved in different clubs rather than burying yourself in a textbook. I think we sometimes forget how much of a privilege it is to experience moments that we'll later look back on so fondly and wish we could relive. Yes, you're going to college for a degree, but you're also going for the people and the network.

Don't get too tied down to the version of yourself on paper — the resume will build with time ♡
